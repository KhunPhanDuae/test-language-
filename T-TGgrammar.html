Pa'O (ပအိုဝ်း) vs. Burmese (ဗမာ) Grammar Structure

This page helps you understand the basic sentence structure differences between Pa'O and Burmese, especially in word order (Subject-Verb-Object).

Burmese (ဗမာ) Grammar: S + O + V (Subject + Object + Verb)

Most Burmese sentences follow a Subject-Object-Verb order. The verb typically comes at the very end of the sentence.

Examples:

ငါ (S) ထမင်း (O) စားတယ် (V)။ I (S) rice (O) eat (V).
မင်း (S) ရေ (O) သောက်လား (V)? You (S) water (O) drink (V)?

Pa'O (ပအိုဝ်း) Grammar: S + V + O (Subject + Verb + Object)

Pa'O sentences often follow a Subject-Verb-Object order, which is similar to English. The verb comes after the subject and before the object.

Examples:

ခွေ (S) အံႏ (V) ဒဲဉ် (O) နဝ်ꩻသွူ။ I (S) eat (V) rice (O) particle.
နာꩻ (S) အွꩻ (V) ထီ (O) ဟွေး? You (S) drink (V) water (O) particle?
